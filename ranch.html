The Experience

A day at the ranch.

Morning is the windows. Coffee in hand, bare feet on warm floor, the Elk Range filling every frame before you have decided what the day holds.

Afternoons stretch. The hot tub against the meadow. The deck with nothing but thirty-five acres of high grass between you and the ridgeline. A walk to the edge of the property where the land drops away and the valley opens below.

Evenings are the fire pit. The stars at this altitude are not what you are accustomed to. The quiet is not the absence of sound — it is the presence of everything the city drowns out.
