A thousand feet above the valley floor, looking directly south at Mt. Sopris.

The Elk Range fills the western horizon. The Roaring Fork Valley runs below. From this altitude, the light moves differently — it comes up over the eastern ridge and finds the windows before the valley floor has fully woken.

Valinor Ranch holds thirty-five acres of working land above the tree line, offered to a single group at a time. The premise is simple: that the right structure, set in the right place, can put nothing between you and the mountains.

The Home

Engineered for the land it stands on.

The home is a shipping container conversion — two structures joined by a glass bridge, oriented to hold the sight line from Mt. Sopris to the Elk Range. The architecture makes no effort to hide what it is. The steel reads against the meadow. The glass holds the mountain in every interior.

Inside, the rooms are spare and deliberate. Wide plank floors, open plan, the windows doing most of the work. The structure earns its place by getting out of the way.

The Seasons

The ranch holds a different quality
in every season.

Winter — Dec – Feb

Frozen silence at altitude. The ranch sits above the valley fog. Some mornings the summit of Mt. Sopris catches the sun before anything below it does.

Summer — Jun – Aug

Long evenings at 6,800 feet. The high meadow grass turns the color of wheat. The Roaring Fork runs cold and full. The afternoon storms build to the south and pass over the ridge.

Autumn — Sep – Nov

The aspen groves on the slopes below turn from green to gold to bare in three weeks. The air sharpens. The light gets long and amber. The elk move through the lower pasture at dusk.
